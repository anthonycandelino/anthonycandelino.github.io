Application Developer at Sun Life Financial

About the Company

In the complex and innovative finance industry, Sun Life Financial is one of the largest and oldest financial services companies in the world. What started as a life insurance company back in 1865 has now blown up into a massive institution specializing in nearly everything to do with customers and their finances. Their slogan, "Life's brighter under the sun" encapsulates their main missions which include leadership in the financial sector, innovation towards tapping into new markets, and sustainability not just in their portfolio but in their environmental impacts as well. Sun Life understands that not everyone has the time and expertise to be maintaining their finances and investments, which is why they supply advisors and their abilities to help those when they need it. No matter your financial goals, Sun Life is with you every step of the way to allow you to live your life your way.

Working at (and away from) Sun Life

Sun Life was one of the most interesting environments I have worked in so far. Never before was I working in such a large office building alongside so many team members. On the first day, the company took over 50 co-ops (just at my office) and got us together to meet each other. With such a daunting office space with hundreds of people, it was great to have people my age to be able to relate and connect with from the get-go. One of the people I was with even happened to work on my floor and collaborated with me on a few projects as team members. Within the first day, I was already comfortable with all the welcoming team members around me and knew for the most part what type of work I would be contributing to in my short four-month period. Whenever I needed assistance there was always someone on my team who could guide me through issues, even when it came time for remote work. The hardest part about the whole company going remote is you never really know what's going to happen next. Even with this though, daily calls and meetings throughout my last month and a half made it so easy to be able to get help and contribute my work. I even felt at times I was more productive from a home environment which was a different experience for sure.

Goals for my Work Term

Sun Life was my first semi-remote work term experience, so I felt as though it was going to be a great opportunity for me to learn new things and prepare for the future. Due to this, I decided that setting four key goals for my upcoming work-term would be beneficial for myself and my work.

" Integrate into my work environment and learn the traits of their corporate culture"

Sun Life was truly my first insight into the inner workings of corporate culture. With each decision made, thousands of customers and employee's lives were most likely affected whether they liked it or not which opened my eyes as to why things were more strict than some of the other work I have been accustomed to. I feel that even though I didn't get a full four months at the office, the corporate culture was very welcoming and allowed me to adapt myself to fit in well whether it was online or at my desk.

" Lead development of new features and projects for my team"

One of the main projects I worked on during my time at Sun Life was a solo project where I was upgrading their health check system for all of the app services they managed. This project for me was a great experience because not only did it allow me to solve problems myself but it also provided me with the opportunity to be able to take a functioning project and upgrade it to a more modern solution. My main goal for this project was to take what was already there and make it easier to use for anyone at the company and from beginning to end I felt I accomplished just that.

" Deepen understanding of object-oriented programming and Spring framework"

While in school, we touch on OOP from time to time but never in the applied ways you see on a co-op term. The work I completed at Sun Life involved mostly all Java and Spring which are two technologies I've wanted to try and improve on for future opportunities. With all the work I completed, I feel I improved immensely in my Java knowledge and got a great first impression working with the Spring Framework. In my next co-op, I will be using both these technologies again so I am glad to have this experience beforehand.

" Overcome challenges of facing new technologies and work environment"

As stated above, this was truly a unique work environment for me. Not only did I see new technologies that I wasn't accustomed to, but I also worked in different and even remote work environments. To me being able to adapt to all these new ways of working was great and gave me great experience for the future. I learned early how quickly work can change and how I needed to be ready to adjust how I work to continue to deliver.

My role as an Application Developer

During my four months of working for Sun Life, most of my time was spent working on a project to upgrade the company's health check for its microservices used in all of their applications. The main goal of this project was to take the bare-bones request URLs that were there and upgrade the system with an overhauled user interface and new features to enable and disable services. The most interesting part about this project was I got to lead the whole development cycle of a project that would be used by countless people after I was done my term so I wanted to ensure I gave it my best effort and asked lots of questions along the way.

For the most part, my job revolved around using Java and Spring Boot which were two things I had very minimal experience with in a work environment. While I had the Object-Oriented experience from school and personal projects I never applied it in the ways that I did at Sun Life. Due to my limited experience with these two tools, I was a bit worried about the length of the learning curve and my ability to be able to make a lasting impact on the company's software. However, from day one the team was great at guiding me through roadblocks and being able to get me up to speed with the projects and tools, I'd be using.

After getting comfortable with my toolset and the company's applications I'd be working on, I was some minor fixes to get me comfortable with making changes on such large applications and testing them to ensure they would work. Once they knew I was comfortable with their apps I began my term project which I described above. When I was at the office, I took advantage of having my team members surrounding me for all my questions but once things got remote being productive and getting help became a lot more difficult. However, with our daily standup meetings, I always got the help I needed and by the end of the term, they couldn't have been happier with the work I had completed. While I only worked with a few key employees they always made sure to set aside time to assist me in overcoming complexities in the company's software system. By the time the last week came around I gave an online demo to numerous team members so they could see how this would improve their workflow when testing the stability of microservices. After all the feedback I felt like I accomplished my term project which truly was a great feeling. Another great thing is many of the tools I learned to use I actually will end up using at my next co-op so to me that's a bonus. It was a truly great learning experience to be a part of a meaningful project that other teams in Sun Life will be using in the future.
