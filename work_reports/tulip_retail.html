Test Automation Developer at Tulip Retail

About the Company

In the age of e-commerce Tulip strived to take a different approach in retail by building the world’s first cloud-based retail platform made exclusively for store associates. Tulip bloomed in 2013 out of a team of developers from Well.ca, a health and beauty e-commerce retailer, who wanted to deliver a meaningful experience to customers within physical stores through software. Utilizing a partnership with Apple, Tulip is taking a world leading mobile device and put it in the hands of store associates to simplify and enhance the in-store experience. Whether it's through assisted selling, endless aisle, customer communication, or POS, Tulip aims to deliver an unparalleled omnichannel shopping experience with clients including: Estée Lauder, Saks Fifth Avenue, Chanel, and Michael Kors to name a few.

My role as aTest Automation Developer

During my 4 months working for Tulip a majority of my time was spent working on a project to replace hundreds of end-user manual test cases with automated scripts that would run nightly on Tulips iOS/Web applications. The main goal of our team was to reduce as many manual test cases as possible in an effort to reduce the time and money needed to test a product before its release. One of the most interesting parts about my job was I got to see firsthand a project being developed from concept to production and by the end of my term we even got our first client team involved with using our suite of scripts.

This being my first co-op experience there was a bit of a learning curve with the stack that Tulip uses. For the most part my job revolved around using Git and JavaScript which were 2 things I had very minimal experience with from my school work. Git was something that was always recommended to me by peers to use for my school work but I never understood it's true use-case until this job. While there was a lot of JavaScript being used it was not basic loops and functions. We used an automation framework CodeceptJS, that was extended to work on Tulip's hybrid iOS/Web application using helpers from Appium and WebdriverIO, other automation frameworks. Before starting this job I was worried about using new technology but I am glad I had to reach out of my comfort zone and try some new things as it taught me some valuable skills I will be using in the future.

After getting comfortable with automation and Tulip's product offering I was assigned to work on writing scripts for automating testing on specific features of the application. My daily work was quite repetitive but because of the vast amount of features to be covered I was always working on something different. Me and my team would have a daily scrum to discuss what we worked on the day before and what we were working on today. This ensured we always knew what everyone was working on and if we needed help who could be of assistance. Our technical lead was an outsourced worker from Belarus so it was often difficult to get help throughout the day so these morning scrums were helpful in clearing up issues from the day before. In the final week of my work at Tulip our team cut our first release branch for our project. This was one of the biggest achievements my team had in the time I was there as it showed that all our work was well out of the early concept stages it was in months prior. It was truly a great learning experience to be apart of a meaningful project that other teams in Tulip will be using in the future.
