Test Automation Developer at Tulip Retail

About the Company

In the age of e-commerce, Tulip strived to take a different approach in retail by building the world’s first cloud-based retail platform made exclusively for store associates. Tulip bloomed in 2013, out of a team of developers from Well.ca, a health and beauty e-commerce retailer, who wanted to deliver a meaningful experience to customers within physical stores through the use of software. Utilizing a partnership with Apple, Tulip was able to take a world leading mobile device and put it in the hands of store associates to simplify and enhance the in-store experience. Whether it's through assisted selling, endless aisle, customer communication, or POS, Tulip delivers an unparalleled omnichannel shopping experience with clients including: Estée Lauder, Saks Fifth Avenue, Chanel, and Michael Kors, to name a few.

Goals for my Work Term

Tulip was my first co-op work term experience, so I felt as though it was going to be a great opportunity for me to learn new things. However, before I began working at Tulip I decided that setting some goals for my upcoming work-term would be beneficial.

" Develop a better understanding of JavaScript"

Coming into Tulip, I didn't have a great amount of experience with JavaScript - aside from one web-development course I had taken on it. After a few days of learning about the software that Tulip had developed, I took it upon myself to learn some JavaScript on the side to expedite the learning process. The hardest part of learning this language was understanding how JavaScript at its core is utilized through Tulip's automation frameworks. However, after some help from other more experienced team members and through my own trial and error, JavaScript became second nature during my final weeks at Tulip. After a completing some online courses on this language and using it daily at work, I can now confidently say I know a lot more about JavaScript than what I knew on the first day of my work term.

" Contribute as a team member to a software project"

My team at Tulip was quite small, therefore I was able to gain some great hands-on work experience with tools and software that I had never seen or worked with previously. Within my first couple of weeks, my team and I had laid out a roadmap to develop a suite of automation scripts that I was able to actively contribute to during my time there. Over the course of my four month work term, my "buddy" and I were the two main developers of these scripts and were able to make great progress towards the final product of them. Our original goal was to reduce manual testing by 33% but after dozens of merge requests we found ourselves reducing it by over 50% , which was quite a massive achievement for our team. Considering that I was a student on my first co-op work-term, it felt great being able to contribute to a big software project that was able to change the future of QA at Tulip.

" Build a network of contacts"

The time I spent at Tulip was my first experience working alongside full-time software developers and business professionals. Consequently, I felt it to be a great opportunity to meet new people and learn a lot about the industry. During my work term, I spoke to many developers and project managers to learn about their career paths and gain access to some valuable advice to help me in my own future endeavors. I also made great friends with other co-op students that I worked with, and I ended up connecting with many of my co-workers on LinkedIn and encouraged them to stay in touch, as I enjoyed my time working with them and found their life experience and advice very interesting and helpful.

My role as aTest Automation Developer

During my four months of working for Tulip, most of my time was spent working on a project to replace hundreds of end-user manual test cases with automated scripts that would run nightly on Tulips iOS/Web applications. The main goal of our team was to reduce as many manual test cases as possible to reduce the time and money needed to test a product before its release. One of the most interesting parts about my job was that I got to see and contribute to a project being developed from concept to production and by the end of my term we even got our first client team involved with using our suite of scripts.

For the most part my job revolved around using Git and JavaScript which were two things I had very minimal experience with from my school work. Git was always recommended to me by peers to use for my school work, but I never understood it's true use-case until my time at Tulip. While there was a lot of JavaScript being used, it was not always basic loops and functions. We often used an automation framework CodeceptJS, that was extended to work on Tulip's hybrid iOS/Web application using helpers from Appium and WebdriverIO; other automation frameworks. Before starting this job I was a bit worried about the length of the learning curve with using new technology, but I am glad I was able to reach out of my comfort zone and try some new technological aspects as it taught me many valuable skills that I will be able to utilize in the future.

After getting comfortable with automation and Tulip's product offering, I was assigned to work on writing scripts for automating testing on specific features of the application. My daily work was quite repetitive but due to the vast number of features to be covered, I was always working on something different. My team and I would have a daily scrum to discuss what we worked on the day before and what we were working on for that day. These daily scrums ensured that our team always knew what everyone was expected to be working on and who could be of assistance if we needed help with our assignments. Our technical lead was an outsourced worker based in Belarus, therefore the time change between Belarus and Ontario made it a bit difficult to get help from him throughout the work day, so these early morning scrums were helpful in clearing up any issues from the day before. In the final week of my work-term at Tulip, our team cut our first release branch for our project. This was one of the biggest achievements my team and I had during my time at Tulip and it was a very proud moment for us as it showed that all of our work was well out of the early concept stages it was in months prior to that moment. It was a truly great learning experience to be a part of a meaningful project that other teams in Tulip will be using in the future.
