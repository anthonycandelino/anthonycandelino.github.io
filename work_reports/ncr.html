Working for NCR

Working for NCR was unlike any other work term I had experienced in the past. The biggest difference for me of course was the remote aspect which for someone who had just finished a previous co-op in a similar fashion was not totally shocking. The pandemic started just before my term and left me a bit worried as to whether it would still be happening but I was assured from the new hire coordinator at NCR that I was still going to be hired and how it was going to work. The first day was a simple pick up of my computer and set up as most start but it was a lot different without having someone nearby to ask when things went wrong. Luckily for me, I was provided more than enough information and even what to do if something had gone wrong which lucky for me it did not. The team dynamic at NCR was much different than what I was used to. I was actively involved in daily meetings to discuss with team members the work we had been doing. I felt this daily communicating helped a lot with keeping me on track and motivated as I always wanted to have something to say I had accomplished the day before. Aside from that while I never saw office space I was consistently invited to meet-ups with other co-ops which helped me keep connected with those I should normally be surrounded by. While I never entered the NCR building, I felt by the end that I did not even need to in order to feel like one of the team. My team lead and new hire coordinators went above and beyond to ensure I was integrated into my team and the NCR family as smoothly and effectively as possible.

Goals for my Work Term

My term at NCR was my first remote work term experience, so I felt as though it was going to be a great opportunity for me to learn new things and prepare for the future. Due to this, I decided that setting four key goals for my upcoming work-term would be beneficial for myself and my work.

" Learn more about Agile and Scrum teamwork"

NCR was truly my first insight into the Agile and Scrum frameworks in an actual work environment. From the initial team meetings, I knew I needed to adjust and learn the process that I would be working with over the course of my term. One of the main things I wanted to focus on is ensuring I understood the process of what to work on and when to assist others and as each sprint went on I felt I was making improvements in my personal process. I also was able to contribute more from a communication standpoint when it came to making decisions and choosing what tasks I would like to work on.

" Learn to work as a good team member in a remote work setting"

I knew that since this was a “remote” work term that I would need to adjust to different formalities when it came to communicating not just my ideas and work but also when it came to getting assistance. From the get-go, I always strived to try to speak as much as I could and contribute however much I could when it came to closing tasks before the end of sprints. In previous roles I know I would be reluctant to ask for guidance when I got hit an obstacle but because people were trusting me to work from home they needed to know when I was unable to.

" Learn to be more independent in problem-solving as I encounter obstacles in the development process"

As stated above, the remote work environment made it more difficult to get assistance from team members. Therefore, I knew that I did not have the luxury of being able to ask them for help on stuff I had already asked about previously. Whenever I got guidance from a team member, I made sure to curate a list of notes that I could reference later so I didn’t need to waste other’s time. As time went on I also learned where I could find a lot of documentation and reading material about projects which often saved me from needing to even ask questions.

" Learn the development process of team and company and contribute to projects according to standards"

With each new placement, I have had brought its own company rules and standards. With a company so invested in financial systems and software I really wanted to ensure I knew what their work processes, coding standards, and documentation interpretations were. Coming in it was a bit daunting as there were many guidelines but in the end, I found them quite beneficial because you always knew exactly what you had to do whether you were updating documentation, closing a story, or beginning work on a completely new project. By the end of my term, I felt I had learned the process to the point where I didn’t need to check to ensure I was completing my work correctly which showed great progress from my initial tasks I worked on.

My role as a Software Developer

This was my first time with the position title of Software Developer, and it was different than the other experiences that I have had. During my time with NCR, we worked in sprint cycles to complete the story's we were given over a project iteration. Due to this fast-paced and agile work environment, I found myself learning about a different one of NCR's projects every 2 weeks. With each sprint, I was assigned to a different and foreign project for me, which made each task I accomplished more challenging but also more rewarding. Nearing the end I found myself quite familiar with getting started on most of the work I was assigned but feel had I worked at the office instead of remote I would have gotten the hang of things a lot sooner.

As far as tasks go, I was given quite a big responsibility within my first weeks of working with my team. I was put in charge of generalizing and moving the integration test suite of a single project into its own separate project so it could be utilized as a dependency for numerous other projects in NCR's system. Considering this was my first real task it was quite challenging but also very rewarding, especially as I got feedback throughout the process.

After my first month, there was a huge reshuffle of teams due to the pandemic and deadlines that needed to be met by the end of summer. I went from just working solo to within an agile team environment. This for me was something that I found very interesting as I had yet to work using sprints and product iterations. The timing was great considering the iteration was 12 weeks long and lasted just in time for my term to end. It took some time to get used to, but I definitely found my ground as each sprint progressed. I started with basic tasks like adding integration tests to projects using the framework I refactored, but by the end of my term, I was changing APIs that affected software people used every day.

Conclusion

I recall when I was first offered the role that I was told that when you work for NCR you aren't "Just a co-op, you will contribute to meaningful work that people use every day". Usually, when you see something like this you may be a bit skeptical thinking it was a marketing tool but I can truly say this is one of those co-op's where you don't feel like the intern on your team. I found myself often explaining to new and existing team members the work I was doing and how it would benefit what we were trying to achieve. This term was truly was a meaningful and important job that prepared me well for my future in a similar field and opened my eyes to a new sector of tech that I hadn't considered wanting to be interested in before.
